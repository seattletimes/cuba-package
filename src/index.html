About the journalists

Ángel González is a business reporter at The Seattle Times, where he closely tracks Amazon.com. Born in Caracas, Venezuela, he’s a graduate of the French Press Institute at University of Paris 2 and has master’s degrees in journalism and Latin American studies from the University of California, Berkeley. He’s a former bureau chief of Dow Jones in Houston, where he covered stories ranging from the BP oil spill to the death of Hugo Chávez. He’s a proud resident of Redmond, where he lives with his Texan wife, Jenny, and sons Rodrigo and Diego. Twitter: @gonzalezseattle. Email: agonzalez@seattletimes.com.

Bettina Hansen is a staff photographer at The Seattle Times and joined the newsroom in 2012. She is a native of Phoenix and attended journalism school at Arizona State University. Before coming to Seattle, she was a staff photographer at the nation’s oldest continuously published newspaper, The Hartford Courant, in Connecticut. She shoots a mix of sports, news and features and is always looking for a new community story. Find her on Twitter and Instagram @bettinahansen or send email to bhansen@seattletimes.com.
